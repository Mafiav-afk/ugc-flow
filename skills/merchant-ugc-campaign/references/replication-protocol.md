# Reference Ad Replication Protocol

## 1. Extract before writing

Record these fields from the reference:

1. Platform, aspect ratio, duration, caption style, and commerce surface.
2. First visible frame and first spoken/text hook.
3. Question planted in the first 1–3 seconds.
4. Share emotion: discovery, disbelief, comedy, identity, relief, aspiration, or proof.
5. Product reveal timestamp and whether it is punchline, background, or pitch.
6. Beat list with exact duration, framing class, action, dialogue, sound, and transition.
7. Proof order: visual demo, texture, result, comparison, review, specification, or offer.
8. Camera grammar: selfie, POV, tripod, macro, screen record, overhead, or mixed.
9. Creator role: peer, expert, owner, tester, skeptic, parent, partner, or mascot.
10. CTA pressure: none, soft discovery, search cue, comment cue, offer, or direct purchase.
11. Repeatable variable slots.
12. Protected elements that must be replaced.

## 2. Build a beat-function map

Label every beat by function rather than surface wording:

`interrupt → curiosity → reveal → setup → hero proof → objection removal → secondary proof → CTA`

Keep the function and timing. Replace the creative expression.

## 3. Similarity controls

Allowed to retain:

- duration and pacing;
- shot type and transition category;
- proof ordering;
- on-camera/voiceover alternation;
- emotional curve and CTA softness.

Always replace:

- creator identity or likeness;
- exact dialogue, captions, jokes, and slogans;
- competitor brand, logo, label, packaging, music, and trademarked character;
- unverifiable results, prices, sales, testimonials, and certifications.

## 4. Replication output

Return a table with: reference beat, function, preserved mechanic, replaced expression, new product execution, and compliance note. Then produce the new 8-beat plan.

## 5. Fidelity check

Confirm that a viewer would recognize the same *format* but not mistake the output for the same brand, creator, or exact advertisement.
